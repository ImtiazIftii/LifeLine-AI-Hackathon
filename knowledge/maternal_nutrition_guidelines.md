# Maternal Nutrition Guidance For LifeLine AI Demo

This local guidance is educational decision-support for pregnancy nutrition. It does not diagnose and does not replace doctors or registered dietitians.

## Anemia And Low Hemoglobin

If hemoglobin is low, or dizziness or weakness is present, prioritize iron-rich foods such as lentils, spinach, small fish, egg, and liver if culturally acceptable and approved by a clinician. Add vitamin C sources such as lemon or guava with meals to support iron absorption. Severe dizziness, fainting, breathlessness, bleeding, or very low hemoglobin needs medical care.

## High Blood Pressure

If blood pressure is high, reduce excess salt, salty pickles, processed snacks, and instant packaged foods. If high blood pressure is present with headache, swelling, or blurred vision, treat it as a danger-sign pattern and seek urgent medical evaluation. Nutrition advice alone is not enough.

## Nausea Or Vomiting

For nausea or vomiting, suggest small frequent meals, bland foods such as rice, roti, banana, and fluids. Avoid oily, spicy, or strong-smelling foods if they worsen nausea. Severe vomiting, inability to keep fluids down, dehydration, or weakness requires medical care.

## Constipation

For constipation, prioritize fiber-rich foods such as lentils, vegetables, guava, ripe papaya, and whole grains. Encourage water and gentle activity if safe. Severe abdominal pain, bleeding, or persistent constipation requires clinician review.

## Budget-Aware Bangladesh Meal Pattern

Low-budget rice-based meals can use rice, dal, spinach or mixed vegetables, egg, small fish, guava, banana, lemon, and safe drinking water. Medium-budget plans may add chicken, more fruit variety, and extra protein portions.

## Safety Reminder

Nutrition suggestions must be reviewed when there are danger signs. Emergency symptoms include bleeding, fainting, breathlessness, severe headache, swelling with high blood pressure, blurred vision, severe vomiting, seizure, or severe abdominal pain.
